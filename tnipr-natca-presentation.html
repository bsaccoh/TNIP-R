Executive Summary

Platform overview

TNIP‑R replaces operator self‑reporting with independent measurement, combining three evidence sources — operator counter data, regulator drive tests and verified citizen complaints — into a single quality‑of‑service picture.

Drive testing & coverage

Independent field campaigns mapped street‑by‑street: coverage, quality, throughput and dead zones per operator and technology.

Reporting & AI

Section 1
Performance Management

KPI computation from operator PM data

Ingestion & computation pipeline

Two Huawei formats are supported — binary pmresult and named CSV — with technology and granularity (15‑min, 30‑min, hourly) detected automatically per file.

Admin‑defined KPI formulas

RRC Setup Success Rate =
100 × {L.RRC.ConnReq.Succ} / NULLIF({L.RRC.ConnReq.Att}, 0)

Validated before activation; supports NULLIF, ABS, MIN, MAX, COALESCE — null‑safe so missing counters skip cleanly.

Live figures from the compliance matrix. Each failure automatically raises a violation alert and can open an enforcement case.

Threshold logic

Thresholds are set per KPI with comparator, required value and warning margin — and can be scoped per operator and per technology (most specific rule wins). Values within the margin raise a WARNING; breaches raise a VIOLATION with an audit‑trailed alert.

Built‑in enforcement workflow

1
Alert

KPI breach detected → violation alert with operator, period, measured vs required value.

2
Notice

Compliance notice issued to the operator from within the platform.

3
Case

Escalation to enforcement case — disputes, submission cycles and licence obligations tracked.

4
Penalty

Penalties & fines module closes the loop with a full documentary record.

Section 5
Operators & Quality of Experience

Per‑operator dashboards and comparison

Operator QoS radar · drive‑test derived

Africell Orange Qcell

Per‑operator command view

Each operator has a dedicated dashboard: KPI trends, compliance history, drive‑test scores, regional breakdowns and complaint volumes — the full quality picture on one screen for licence reviews.

Network KPIs vs experienced QoE

Network KPIs show what the equipment did; QoE data — complaint streams, call‑drop reports, throughput on real handsets — shows what the citizen experienced. Both sit on the same axis, so a "green" network with dissatisfied subscribers is flagged for investigation.

Regional analysis

QoS scores and coverage compliance per province — Western Area, Northern, Southern, Eastern — sample‑weighted, making rural service gaps visible alongside Freetown's figures.

Drive tests measure the network a few days a year; citizens experience it every minute. The QoS Complaint application gives every verified subscriber a structured channel to the regulator, and gives NatCA a continuous, geolocated stream of quality‑of‑experience data.
